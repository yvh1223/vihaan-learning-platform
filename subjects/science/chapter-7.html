üìù Key Terms

  • Food chain: A single path of energy from producer to consumer.
  • Food web: Many connected food chains in one ecosystem.
  • Population: All members of one species in an area.
  • Symbiosis: A close relationship between two species.
  • Balance: When resources and populations are stable.

Chapter 7 Study Guide

Summarize how ecosystems stay balanced and how living things adapt to change.

Key takeaways

  • Energy starts with producers, moves through consumers, and ends with decomposers recycling nutrients.
  • Food webs map many energy paths; removing one species can ripple through the whole web.
  • Symbiosis includes mutualism, commensalism, parasitism, and competition, each affecting survival.
  • Environmental changes (drought, wildfire, invasive species, pollution) challenge balance, but populations can adjust.

Vocabulary to master

  • Food web: A system of multiple food chains showing how energy flows.
  • Symbiosis: Any close relationship between two species.
  • Population: All individuals of a species living in one place.
  • Migration: Seasonal movement to find food or shelter.
  • Balance: When species and resources are healthy and steady.

Study moves

  1. Trace energy through one food web diagram and describe how a change affects every level.
  2. Match real-world examples to the four types of symbiosis to see how species interact.
  3. Adjust rabbit and fox numbers in your head and predict how the ecosystem would respond.

Questions to answer

  1. What happens to foxes if there are far fewer rabbits?
  2. How can invasive species throw a habitat out of balance?
  3. Why are decomposers still important when a wildfire happens?
