Chapter 5

Westward Expansion & Pioneer Life

Manifest Destiny, Oregon Trail, California Gold Rush, and the challenges of frontier life.

Unit Overview

Investigate why people moved west, the routes they took, and how expansion changed land, economies, and communities.

Big Ideas

  • Migration happens because of push and pull factors.
  • Trails, rivers, and railroads shaped where people traveled.
  • Westward expansion brought opportunity and conflict.
  • Indigenous nations were deeply impacted by new settlers.

Skills You Will Practice

  • Map routes like the Oregon, Santa Fe, and California trails.
  • Compare perspectives of pioneers, settlers, and Indigenous peoples.
  • Explain causes and effects of expansion.
  • Analyze how technology changed travel and trade.

Essential Questions

  • Why did families choose to move west?
  • How did geography shape the journey?
  • What were the costs and benefits of expansion?

üöö Push and Pull Factors

People moved west for many reasons. Some were pushed by problems at home, while others were pulled by new opportunities.

Push Factors

  • Limited farmland or jobs in the East.
  • Economic hard times and debt.
  • Overcrowded cities.

Pull Factors

  • Available land for farming and ranching.
  • Gold discoveries in California.
  • New towns, trade, and business opportunities.

üåæ Impact on Indigenous Peoples

Westward expansion brought new settlers onto Indigenous lands, leading to broken treaties, forced relocation, and loss of resources.

  • Some nations resisted; others negotiated to protect their people.
  • Buffalo herds declined as railroads and hunting expanded.
  • Policies like reservations changed how people could live.
