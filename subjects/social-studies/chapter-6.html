Chapter 6

Civil War Era

Causes of the Civil War, major battles, key figures, and the impact on the nation.

Unit Overview

Learn why the nation divided, what happened during the Civil War, and how the conflict changed the United States.

Big Ideas

  • Disagreements over slavery and states' rights led to secession.
  • The Union and Confederacy fought a long and costly war.
  • Key battles and leaders influenced the war's outcome.
  • The Civil War ended slavery and reshaped the nation.

Skills You Will Practice

  • Sequence events on a Civil War timeline.
  • Identify causes, turning points, and effects.
  • Compare life on the home front in North and South.
  • Use evidence to explain historical decisions.

Essential Questions

  • What caused the Civil War?
  • How did key battles change the war?
  • How did the war transform American society?

‚öñÔ∏è Causes of the Civil War

  • Slavery: The most serious disagreement between North and South.
  • States' Rights: Debates about how much power states should have.
  • Economy: Industrial North and farming South had different needs.
  • Expansion: Conflict over whether new states would allow slavery.
  • Sectionalism: Different regions began to see themselves as separate.
  • Election of 1860: Abraham Lincoln's victory pushed southern states to secede.

üó∫Ô∏è Key Events and Turning Points

  • Fort Sumter: First shots of the war (1861).
  • Antietam: Bloodiest single-day battle; led to the Emancipation Proclamation (1862).
  • Gettysburg: Major Union victory and turning point (1863).
  • Vicksburg: Union gains control of the Mississippi River (1863).
  • Appomattox Court House: General Lee surrenders (1865).

üïäÔ∏è Emancipation and the 13th Amendment

The Emancipation Proclamation declared freedom for enslaved people in Confederate states. The 13th Amendment later ended slavery in the United States.

  • Enslaved people used the war to pursue freedom in many ways, including escaping to Union lines.
  • After 1863, many African American men served in Union regiments.

üß≠ Key Figures

Union Leaders

  • Abraham Lincoln: President who preserved the Union.
  • Ulysses S. Grant: Union general who led the final campaigns.
  • Clara Barton: Nurse who improved battlefield care.

Confederate Leaders

  • Jefferson Davis: President of the Confederacy.
  • Robert E. Lee: Leading Confederate general.

Voices for Freedom

  • Frederick Douglass: Abolitionist who argued for equality.
  • Harriet Tubman: Helped people escape slavery and aided the Union.

üè† Life on the Home Front

The war touched civilians on both sides. Families adjusted to shortages, new roles, and uncertainty.

  • Women managed farms, businesses, and households while many men fought.
  • Drafts were used to fill armies, especially as the war continued.
  • Both sides faced shortages; inflation rose in the Confederacy.
  • Communities held rallies and raised money for supplies and hospitals.

ü©∫ Battlefield Medicine

Medical care improved during the war, but many soldiers still faced disease and infection.

  • Doctors performed amputations to prevent infections from spreading.
  • Nurses organized field hospitals and improved sanitation.
  • Disease caused more deaths than battlefield wounds.
