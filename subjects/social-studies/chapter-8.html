üèôÔ∏è Progressive Reforms

The Progressive Era focused on fixing problems caused by rapid industrial growth.

Goals of Reform

  • Protect workers with safer conditions and shorter hours.
  • Clean up city services like water, trash, and transportation.
  • Make government more honest and responsive.
  • Conserve forests, parks, and natural resources.

Voices for Change

  • Muckrakers wrote about unsafe factories and unfair business practices.
  • Settlement houses helped immigrant families in cities.
  • The 19th Amendment granted women the right to vote.

ü™ñ World War I and the Home Front

Global alliances pulled many countries into war, and the United States joined in 1917.

Causes of the War

  • Alliances meant a conflict in one area spread quickly.
  • Competition for land and power raised tensions.
  • New military technology made battles deadly.

Home Front Actions

  • Families rationed food and bought war bonds.
  • Factories shifted to produce uniforms, ships, and supplies.
  • Women took more factory and office jobs during the war.
  • Soldiers and nurses served overseas in difficult conditions.
  • The draft required many young men to serve.

üåá The 1920s and the Crash

After the war, new inventions and entertainment changed daily life.

  • Cars, radios, and movies connected people and spread new ideas.
  • Some families prospered, but many farmers struggled with low prices.
  • Speculation in the stock market led to a major crash in 1929.

üìâ The Great Depression

The economic collapse hurt families, businesses, and farmers across the country.

Causes & Challenges

  • Businesses closed and unemployment rose sharply.
  • Bank failures wiped out savings.
  • The Dust Bowl damaged farms in the Great Plains.

New Deal Responses

  • Programs like the CCC and WPA created jobs.
  • New rules protected banks and workers.
  • Communities built parks, roads, and schools.
